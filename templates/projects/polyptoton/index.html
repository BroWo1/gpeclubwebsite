Polyptoton

By: Andy Wang

Definition

Polyptoton is a figure of speech that repeats words sharing the same root.

Definition: Root Words

Root words are the basic parts of words that can be combined with affixes to form other words.

Note: Root words are not always standalone words.

Example of Root Words

Root Word Definition Formed Words
port carry Transport, import, portal
cycl circle Cycle, bicycle, encyclopedia
ego I Egocentric, superego

Why Use Polyptoton?

  • Emphasize a certain meaning that is typically related to the root word repeated in the polyptoton.
  • Add rhythm to the sentence (commonly used in poetry and speeches).
  • Are often used to convey messages rather obscurely.

Examples

“Who shall watch the watchmen?”
The sentence repeats words of the root word “watch” (meaning see), which emphasizes watching to convey an ironic message (in the context) that nobody watches over the watchmen.

“You shall not pass before I bleed till my blood dries.”
The sentence repeated words with the root word blōd (meaning blood) to emphasize unstop bleeding, which further conveys the meaning of death in the context. The emphasis of unstopped bleeding further tints the sentence with a dramatic emotion, typically rendering the determined thought of not letting “you” pass.

“With eager feeding food doth choke the feeder.” --Shakespeare
The sentence repeats words of the root “feed” to emphasize the meaning of feeding too much is bad, which further extends to contextual metaphors.

How to Write a Polyptoton?

  1. Convey your sentence intuitively: You shall not pass before I die.
  2. Identify a root word that relates with your meaning: bleed (blōd) is a word related to death.
  3. Think of an expression repeating your chosen root word to replace the intuitive expression: “bleed till my blood dries” could be used to replace “die”.

You shall not pass before I bleed till my blood dries.
